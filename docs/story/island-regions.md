# Island Regions And Flow

## Region-by-Region Progression (Metroidvania Flow)

1. Waking Coast (start) -> Tidefen Village hub.
2. Branch A: Low Canopy Jungle -> Smuggler Inlet and Pirate Fort (first dungeon).
3. Branch B: Emberroot Village -> Underroot Cave System (unlock: double jump).
4. Return pass: backtrack to Jungle shrine locks and elevated canopy routes.
5. Midgame ring: High Canopy Jungle -> Observatory Ridge -> Glassmire Village.
6. Tower path: Stormwatch Tower (unlock: arcane seal key).
7. Endgame ring: Ironwake Village -> Castle Approach -> Blackreef Castle.
8. Final descent: Book vault beneath Blackreef.

## Metroidvania Layout Rules

- Villages are safe hubs connected by dangerous spoke regions.
- Every major branch must loop back to at least one earlier hub.
- Ability unlocks open at least two old gates, not just one new path.
- Vertical lanes (caves/canopy/tower) should reconnect to horizontal lanes (villages/coast/fort).
- Endgame areas should be reachable from multiple late-game hubs.

## Gate Legend

- [DJ-1/2/3]: double jump gate tiers
- [AK]: arcane key gate from Stormwatch Tower
- LIFT: one-way shortcut loop that reconnects a dungeon branch to a hub

## Region Backstories

### Waking Coast

Once the ceremonial landing site for wardens sworn to protect forbidden knowledge, this coast is where all oaths began. During the Memory Tempests, the tide carried shattered oath-stones back to shore, and each storm stripped names from the carvings. Vael waking here is viewed by locals as either fate or a bad omen, because only oathbound blood is said to survive the black surf.

### Tidefen Village (V1)

Tidefen was built by fisher-families who abandoned deep water after seeing lights beneath the waves. They became practical salvagers and mapmakers, trading safe routes between storms. The village now survives by strict bell curfews and memory-knot rituals, but fear is rising because Ilyr's scouts have started buying old charts to the inner island.

### Low Canopy Jungle

This lower jungle was once a managed medicine garden maintained by shrine keepers. When the wardens fell, roots swallowed the terraces and alchemical runoff changed the wildlife into aggressive territorial packs. Beneath the vines are broken reliefs showing the Book of All being carried inland under heavy guard.

### Smuggler Inlet

Originally a hidden supply harbor for the wardens, the inlet became a black-market crossroads after the seals fractured. Smugglers learned to move cargo only during thunder squalls, when memory echoes are weakest. Many still believe there is a chained service tunnel from the inlet to the castle's underworks.

### Pirate Fort

The fort began as a naval watchtower, then was seized by exiled corsairs who repurposed its signal mirrors for raids. Its current crews sell relics, prisoners, and false maps while pretending neutrality between villages. Ilyr's early funding came from a deal struck here: arcane protection in exchange for access to deep vault routes.

### Emberroot Village (V2)

Emberroot sits on mineral-hot ground and grew around old forges that once made ceremonial warden tools. The smith clans are proud, stubborn, and deeply split over whether the island should be resealed or conquered. They distrust outsiders, but they respect proof of strength, making Emberroot a likely place for Vael to earn allies.

### Underroot Cave System

These caves are the island's oldest foundations, carved by underground rivers and later etched with warding script. The deeper chambers act like memory resonators, replaying moments from anyone who passes through. Vael's double jump relic trial belongs here because the caves were once used to train wardens to move in low-gravity arcane currents.

### Relic Chamber

This chamber housed movement relics used by guardian initiates, including the sigil that grants double jump. It was sealed after an apprentice miscast a levitation rite and collapsed an entire hall. The chamber now tests intent as much as skill, and it refuses to awaken for those seeking power without purpose.

### High Canopy Jungle

The upper jungle formed above collapsed ruins, creating a second world suspended in roots and stone bridges. Scout clans once used this canopy as a silent courier highway between villages. Today it is full of blind predators and old warden zipline anchors, ideal for high-mobility traversal and hidden backtrack rewards.

### Canopy Shaft

This vertical cut connects lower and upper jungle layers through a collapsed watch elevator. It used to be a guarded checkpoint where wardens inspected relic transport. After the fall, the shaft became a proving route, and surviving marks on the walls track the names of those who failed the climb.

### Observatory Ridge

The ridge observatory tracked celestial tides believed to influence memory erosion. Its astronomer-priests predicted the current catastrophe but were ignored by political leaders seeking the Book. The surviving instruments can still align hidden pathways, but each activation also broadcasts a signal that draws hostile arcane entities.

### North Shoals

These windswept cliffs and tidal shelves were once used for long-range beacon communication. Most beacons are dead, but one still flashes on storm nights with no known operator. Villagers say it is either a trapped warden spirit or an automated warning from the island itself.

### Floating Causeway

This suspended transit line was engineered with anti-gravity anchors to move relics safely above hostile ground. After the island fractured, sections broke apart and drifted, creating intermittent traversal windows. Reactivating the causeway is both a navigation breakthrough and a strategic declaration to all factions.

### Glassmire Village (V3)

Glassmire was founded by scholars who fled political courts and built a town around mirrored wetlands. They preserve oral histories in layered chant to avoid written corruption from memory loss. Their elders know the strongest truth about Vael's past, but they release it only when trust and timing align.

### Stormwatch Tower

Originally a weather and ward control spire, the tower became an arcane laboratory after traditional wardens died out. Ilyr chose it as his operational base because it amplifies ritual precision and can project lock-breaking frequencies across the island. The arcane key obtained here is less a key than a tuning pattern that destabilizes old seal geometry.

### Ironwake Village (V4)

Ironwake began as a military logistics camp and hardened into a frontier stronghold. Its people are disciplined and survival-focused, valuing duty over myth. They stand closest to the castle threat and are prepared to burn their own district rather than let dark arcane forces spill west.

### Castle Approach

These battlements and processional roads once welcomed sworn guardians entering the final sanctum. The path is now contested by traps, failed constructs, and rival faction strike teams. Architecturally, it is designed to exhaust invaders before they ever reach the inner gates.

### Blackreef Castle

The castle was never a royal home; it was a prison-temple built to contain knowledge judged too dangerous for any age. Its outer halls store sanctioned doctrine, while its inner circles hide forbidden praxis and weaponized memory rites. Anyone trying to claim the Book must cross chambers designed to test motive, not just strength.

### Book Vault

Buried beneath Blackreef, this vault is the final lock in the island's seal network. The Book of All is bound here by layered oaths requiring both bloodline resonance and cognitive endurance. The vault does not merely reveal knowledge; it reflects the seeker's deepest justification and magnifies it.
